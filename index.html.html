MOBILE OPERATIONS CENTER MODULE - SKYGRID

Autonomous Operations for Vegetation Management and Thermal Inspection

1. Executive Summary

Strategic Opportunity

At the request of international strategic partners, the Mobile Operations Center Module explores advanced technologies such as Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) operations, point cloud generation, and full mission automation. These capabilities significantly contribute to increasing operational efficiency, especially in scenarios with difficult access and a scarcity of specialized operators.

Proposed Solution

A mobile operations center consisting of a 4x4 vehicle adapted with an onboard DJI Dock 3. The system can execute fully autonomous missions within a radius of up to 2km, eliminating the need for local technical experts for both drone operation and preliminary data analysis.

Operational Advantage

  • Access to remote areas without the need for a local specialized team.
  • Automated operations that significantly increase productivity.
  • Processing and analysis can be performed remotely.
  • Reduced dependence on multiple professionals in the field.

2. Context & Identified Opportunities

2.1 Technological Direction

We investigated advanced technologies focusing on:

  • Autonomous operations: missions without a dedicated pilot in the field.
  • BVLOS operations: capability to operate beyond the direct line of sight.
  • Point cloud generation: complete 3D digitalization.
  • Automated analysis: data processing without local expert dependency.

These technologies directly address two universal challenges in the electrical sector:

  1. Difficulty of access to infrastructure in remote areas.
  2. Scarcity of technical specialists for field operation and analysis.

2.2 Operational Improvement Opportunities

Thermal Inspection of Substations:

  • Challenge: Limited coverage in unattended substations.
  • Opportunity: Complete and regular thermal mapping.
  • Benefit: Early detection of anomalies without a local specialist.

Vegetation Management in Spans:

  • Challenge: Systematic monitoring of vegetation growth.
  • Opportunity: Automate identification of potential risks.
  • Benefit: Predictive vs. reactive maintenance.

3. Technical Concept

3.1 System Overview and Operational Principle

The Mobile Operations Center enables the digitalization of infrastructure in hard-to-reach areas, removing barriers of physical access and local technical expertise. Complex operations are performed automatically from a mobile point with connectivity.

  1. Vehicle reaches the closest possible point via road.
  2. Automated system performs missions in surrounding inaccessible areas (2km radius).
  3. Data is processed and analyzed remotely.
  4. Eliminates the need for a specialized team in the field.

3.2 Main Components

Mobile Platform

4x4 vehicle (pickup type) with a protected compartment, autonomous power system, and redundant satellite/cellular connectivity.

Autonomous Flight System

DJI Dock 3 with a compatible drone, allowing fully automated operation with a 2km range for multiple daily missions.

Connectivity and Processing

Satellite terminal (Starlink type), onboard processing capability, selective data transmission, and remote analysis by specialists.

4. Use Case 1: Thermal Inspection of Substations

4.1 Context and Opportunity

Unattended or remote substations present a challenge for regular inspection. Automated aerial thermography allows for early identification of thermal anomalies that may indicate developing problems, without requiring the displacement of technical specialists.

4.2 Automated Approach

Operation via Mobile Center:

  • Vehicle is positioned near the substation.
  • Dock executes a pre-programmed thermal mapping mission.
  • Systematic coverage of all equipment.
  • Thermal data is transmitted for remote analysis.

4.3 Processing and Analysis

Capabilities:

  • Capture of high-resolution thermal images.
  • Identification of abnormal temperature gradients.
  • Comparison with previous inspections (trending).
  • Support for predictive maintenance.

4.4 Expected Benefits

Increased productivity in thermal inspections.
Coverage of unattended substations on a regular basis.
Less dependence on local technical specialists.
Early detection of potential problems.
Database for predictive analysis.

5. Use Case 2: Vegetation Management in Spans

5.1 Operational Context

Vegetation management in transmission line spans represents a significant challenge, especially in hard-to-reach areas where regular monitoring is complex and costly. Early detection of potential interferences allows for proper planning of interventions, avoiding emergency situations.

5.2 Technical Solution - Automated Photogrammetry

Approach:

  • Automated capture of images with high overlap.
  • Generation of a point cloud through photogrammetry.
  • Analysis of the distance between vegetation and cables.
  • Identification of areas requiring attention.

Advantage of the Mobile System

  • Access to remote spans without trails or roads.
  • Automated operation eliminates the need for a specialized pilot.
  • Processing can be performed remotely.
  • Georeferenced digital documentation of each span.

5.3 Operational Benefits

  • Increased productivity through automated operations.
  • Coverage of areas inaccessible by traditional methods.
  • Predictive planning based on objective data.
  • Reduced dependence on specialized field teams.
  • Digital history for trend analysis.

5.4 Deliverables

Expected Products:

  • 3D model of the inspected spans.
  • Vegetation-infrastructure proximity mapping.
  • Reports prioritized by criticality.
  • Georeferenced database for management.

6. Regulatory Compliance and BVLOS

6.1 Brazilian Regulatory Context

BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) operation in Brazil is regulated by ANAC through RBAC-E No. 94. The Mobile Operations Center operates within a framework that seeks regulatory compatibility while enabling advanced operations.

6.2 Proven Dronpower History with ANAC

BVLOS Authorization (2023)

Obtained authorization for substation inspection with BVLOS, setting a precedent for autonomous operations within a controlled perimeter of electrical infrastructure.

Specialized Authorization (2021)

Approval for operation with an incendiary device on a transmission line, demonstrating the capability to navigate complex regulatory processes.

6.3 Compliance Strategy for the Module

Operational Approach

  • Operations in rural and controlled areas.
  • Conservative operational radius of 2km.
  • Established safety procedures.
  • Geo-fence and automatic return-to-home.

Risk Mitigation

  • Operator present on-site (in the vehicle).
  • Constant communication with air traffic control where applicable.
  • Specific insurance for operations.
  • Defined emergency protocols.
